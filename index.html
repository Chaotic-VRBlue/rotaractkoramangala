The Four Way Test

The Four-Way Test is one of the hallmarks of Rotary. Since it was developed in 1932 by Herbert J. Taylor, RI President 1954-55. The four brief questions are not based on culture or religion. Instead, they are a simple checklist for ethical behavior. They transcend generations and national borders. As an individual, we should have The Four-Way Test in mind in every decision we make, all day long. Our utmost responsibility is to speak the truth, to be fair, to build goodwill and better friendships, and to do our very best in all situations.

Is it the  TRUTH ?

Is it  FAIR to all concerned?

Will it build  GOODWILL &  BETTER FRIENDSHIPS ?

Will it be  BENEFICIAL to all concerned?
